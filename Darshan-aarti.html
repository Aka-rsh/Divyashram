Temples of Importance in Varanasi

Shri Kashi Vishwanath Temple

Also known as the Golden Temple, it is dedicated to Lord Shiva, the presiding deity of the city. The first Jyotirlinga manifested here, making it the devotional focus of Varanasi.

Maa Annapurna Temple

Near Kashi Vishwanath Temple, dedicated to Devi Annapurna, the Goddess of Food and Nourishment.

Kalbhairav Temple

Ancient temple near Head Post Office. Kalbhairav is the “Kotwal of Varanasi,” who guards the city. No one can stay in Kashi without his permission.

New Vishwanath Temple

Located in Banaras Hindu University premises, built by the Birlas. Open to all, irrespective of caste or creed.

Tulsi Manas Temple

Dedicated to Lord Rama, located where Tulsidas lived and wrote the “Ramcharitmanas.” Verses inscribed on walls.

Sankatmochan Temple

Founded by Goswami Tulsidas, dedicated to Lord Hanuman, the protector from troubles. Known as the “Monkey Temple.”

Durga Temple

Built in the 18th century, known for beautiful stonework and architecture. Goddess Durga symbolizes strength and power.

Bharat Mata Temple

A unique temple dedicated to Mother India, shaped like India map, symbolizing unity and patriotism.
